What is Hackspace?

University at Buffalo's computer science and engineering coworking hackerspace, managed by UB ACM.

The Hackspace is a 30-person room that is dedicated as a computer science and engineering coworking hackerspace. Students are free to use this room during our opening hours for work.



Can I use this place?

Yes! Hackspace, just like our club, is open for all UB students. UB students are free to use this space, as long as the following rules are followed:

  1. Respect others who are using the space for work.
    Keep the room in a quiet and reasonable volume.
  2. Keep the room clean and tidy.
    The Hackspace is not visited by the school custodians. If you have garbage you'd like to throw out, there is a convenient garbage can located right outside the room.

Any infractions will result in a warning or ban from using the Hackspace.

Location

O'Brian 5

We are located in the O'Brian Law School building. Find the staircase on either side of the building and head downstairs to the basement.



Opening Hours

Monday to Friday 9am-9pm*

*The room is unlocked by a keyholder in the morning and locked at closing time.

Want more hacking?

UB ACM runs a biweekly hack session called Hack Nights, where Davis 101 is open for students to work from 1pm-9pm. We serve dinner, offer end-of-semester prizes, and help sponsors recruit, and more!

Find out more at http://ubacm.org/hack.

We are UB ACM, University at Buffalo's undergraduate computer science organization. Our mission is to empower student peers and help them find their path in computer science.

UB ACM hosts a variety of extracurricular events to achieve our goal. We have weekly meetings where our members or speakers run workshops and tech talks on the latest technologies used by professionals in industry or academia. Additionally, we run events like Hack Night and social events to allow students to connect with others with similar interests.
